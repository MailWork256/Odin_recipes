  1. -- Prepare the dough --
    • Mix 10g fresh yeast (or 7g sachet dried yeast) with ½ tsp sugar and 250ml warm water. Let it sit for 10 minutes.
    • Place 375g Italian ‘00’ flour (plus extra for dusting) in a table-top mixer with a dough hook. Pour in the yeast mixture and beat at medium speed for 10 minutes (or mix in a bowl and knead with oiled hands for 5-10 minutes).
    • Leave the dough somewhere warm for 10 more minutes, then add the remaining flour and 1 tbsp olive oil. Beat or knead to form a dough for another 5 minutes.
    • Put the dough in a well-oiled bowl, cover it with a cloth, and let it double in size (approximately 1½ hours).

  2. -- Prepare the tomato sauce --
    • Heat 3 tbsp olive oil in a pan over moderate heat.
    • Add 1 chopped onion and cook for 3 minutes, stirring constantly.
    • Add 1 crushed garlic clove, 1 tsp sea salt, and 1 tsp ground black pepper. Cook for 2 more minutes.
    • Add 2 x 400g cans of good-quality Italian chopped tomatoes and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. -- Shape and top the pizza --
    • Once the dough has doubled in size, slap it down on a lightly floured surface and knead for 4 minutes until soft but not too elastic.
    • Divide the dough into 6 pieces, roll them into balls, and let them rest for 10 minutes.
    • Preheat your oven to the highest temperature possible (around 270°C fan).
    • Roll out each ball of dough as thinly as possible.
    • Remove a flat baking sheet from the oven, oil or dust it with flour, and carefully transfer the dough base onto it.
    • Spread some of the tomato sauce, then add torn mozzarella cheese and any desired toppings (like slices of ham or rocket).

  4. -- Bake the pizza --
    • Cook the pizza for 5-10 minutes, depending on your oven temperature, until the base is crisp and the cheese is melted.
    • Scatter fresh basil over the top.
